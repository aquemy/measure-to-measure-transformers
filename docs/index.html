Measure-to-measure interpolation using Transformers

A machine-checked formalization and numerical validation

This site accompanies a Lean 4 formalization and numerical validation of

M. Geshkovski, P. Rigollet, Y. Ruiz-Balet, Measure-to-measure interpolation using Transformers, arXiv:2411.04551v3.

Who did what. The paper, its theorems, and its proofs are the work of Geshkovski, Rigollet, and Ruiz-Balet. The Lean 4 formalization, the numerical experiments, the prose informalizations, this website, and the blueprint are the work of Alexandre Quemy. Throughout, the original authors’ statements and proofs are reproduced verbatim in clearly marked, page-cited grey boxes, for scholarly comparison; everything in blue or green boxes is the present author’s contribution.

The result, in one paragraph

A Transformer can be viewed as the flow map of a continuity equation on the unit sphere \(\mathbb S^{d-1}\): each token is a particle, and a single time-dependent parameter \(\theta = (\mathbf V, \mathbf B, \mathbf W, \mathbf U, b)\) drives every particle through a self-attention plus perceptron velocity field, projected to the tangent space by layer normalization. The paper proves a controllability result: one such Transformer, with explicit piecewise-constant parameters and a bounded number of switches, can steer \(N\) arbitrary input measures to \(N\) arbitrary target measures, to within \(\varepsilon\) in the Wasserstein distance \(W_2\). The construction is a three-act composition:

  1. Disentangle (Prop 3.1) – a piecewise-constant control separates the \(N\) overlapping supports into pairwise-disjoint regions, using barycenter separation.
  2. Cluster (Prop 2.1 / 2.2) – self-attention contracts each measure to a point (or to \(M\) atoms).
  3. Match (Prop 4.1 / 4.2) – a perceptron gate steers each cluster to its target while parking the others.

Theorem 1.1 handles Dirac targets sharing a missing direction; Theorem 1.2 handles transport-matchable targets.

What this project adds

  • Machine-checked leaves. The self-contained computational cores of the proofs (the gate ODE, the barycenter ODE, the geodesic gradient, the separating hyperplane, the Lyapunov sign, the ball-chain retention, the pigeonhole step, and the disjoint-hulls/non-colinear-barycenters step) are isolated and given clean Lean 4 kernel proofs.
  • A labeled axiom layer. The optimal-transport (\(W_1\), \(W_2\)) and continuity-equation-flow theory missing from Mathlib is isolated as a small, explicit set of named axioms, so every downstream result shows exactly what it rests on under #print axioms.
  • Two corrections/strengthenings. A load-bearing sign error in the gate of Lemma B.2 (F1, see errata), caught by the formalization and an experiment; and a rigor gap in Proposition 3.1’s induction (F2) discharged by a machine-checked leaf.
  • A seeded validation campaign. Seven experiments integrate the actual dynamics and confirm the quantitative content of the claims, with figures and provenance manifests.
