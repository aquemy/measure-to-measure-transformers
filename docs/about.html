What the formalization strengthens

F1 – a sign error in the gate of Lemma B.2

The proof of Lemma B.2 (Appendix B, p. 31, eq. (B.4)) takes parameters \(U = -z\mathbf 1^\top\), \(b = \cos(R)\mathbf 1\), giving the gate \((\cos R - \langle z,x\rangle)_+\), and asserts it is positive exactly on the ball \(\mathcal B_0 = B(z,R)\). But \[\cos R - \langle z,x\rangle > 0 \iff \langle z,x\rangle < \cos R \iff d_g(z,x) > R \iff x \notin \mathcal B_0,\] so the printed gate is active on the complement of \(\mathcal B_0\); as written, the interior mass sees a zero gate and the lemma transports nothing. The fix is a one-sign typo: \(U = +z\mathbf 1^\top\), \(b = -\cos(R)\mathbf 1\). The error is typographical and recoverable – no theorem (Prop 2.2, Theorems 1.1, 1.2) is affected once the sign is flipped.

It is corroborated three ways: (i) the paper’s own Proposition 4.2, Step 3 (p. 22) uses the same gate and states it with the correct (opposite) side; (ii) the kernel-checked leaf MeasureToMeasure.Leaves.gate_pos_iff_dist (L2) proves the gate is active on \(\{d_g(z,x)>R\}\); and (iii) experiment E1 failed (mass fraction \(\approx 0.27\)) when seeding mass inside \(B(z,R)\) as the printed (B.4) suggests, and passed (fraction \(1.0\)) only on the gate-active region L2 identifies.

The full erratum note (written so it can be sent to the authors as a courtesy) is in ERRATA.md.

F2 – a rigor gap in Proposition 3.1’s induction

The induction proving Proposition 3.1 asserts the step “disjoint geodesic hulls \(\Rightarrow\) non-colinear barycenters” without proof. The formalization supplies it as the machine-checked leaf barycenter_noncolinear_of_disjoint_hull (L11) for the empirical regime, and barycenter_noncolinear_of_disjoint_hull_general (L11\('\), via Mathlib’s Convex.integral_mem) for arbitrary probability measures, with no new axioms.

Honesty: what is proved, axiomatised, or open

Every node carries a status badge driven by the Lean kernel (#print axioms, via bin/axiom-report):

  • machine-checked – clean kernel proof (propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound only);
  • axiomatised – rests on a labeled axiom standing in for mean-field / dynamical theory missing from Mathlib;
  • open – stated in Lean as a sorry;
  • informal – reviewed on paper, not yet stated in Lean.

A node is never badged above its true kernel status. The two headline theorems are proved by assembling the mid-level results over the axiom layer (zero sorry), so their effective status is axiomatised – clean on their own logic, but resting on the labeled mean-field / dynamical axioms. Kernel honesty is necessary but not sufficient: the kernel checks each statement as written, never that it faithfully transcribes the paper. So every axiom additionally passes a statement-fidelity admission protocol (verbatim source anchor, hypothesis-parity diff, adversarial refutation attempt, model-adequacy check; Source-Ref: / Refutation-Attempt: commit footers per CKC spec v0.2.0, enforced by the pre-commit hook) and is guarded by a committed refutation regression suite: the disproofs of previously-refuted draft statements are kernel-checked theorems, paired with must-fail adapters that CI compiles on every push, and every axiom carries a compiled non-vacuity witness (concrete data satisfying all its hypotheses), so an over-strengthened statement breaks the build just as a re-loosened one trips the gate. This audit (findings F11–F20 in RESEARCH.md) caught two axioms that were not merely loose but unsound – lemma_5_1 (F19, a Measure.map measurability escape) and meanFieldFlow_unique (F20, false off the sphere) – each realigned with the paper’s actual hypotheses and then discharged to a machine-checked theorem. The self-contained leaves and the whole Appendix-B transport chain carry the machine-checked load; see Beyond Mathlib for the plan to discharge the remaining axioms and lift the headline results to machine-checked.
